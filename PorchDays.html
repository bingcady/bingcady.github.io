"It's good to see you, Beth."

"Nice to see you, Max."

"How long has it been?"

"What do you mean?"

"How long has it been since we've seen each other?"

"About three days. Why?"

"So the sun's gone up and down three times since then."

"Uh-huh."

"Where've you been?"

"Standing right over there."

"Really? For the whole three days?"

"Yes, Max. I was lost, and they tell you if you're lost to stay there and your mother will find you."

"But that only applies to department stores, Beth."

"Really?"

"Well, did it work? Did she find you?"

"Of course not, Max. My mother lives in a hut, with eskimos. New Mexico somewhere."

"But still..."

"Why are you bothering me, Max?"

All right. If you must know, I'm trying to set the world record."

"Really? How many do you have left to go?"

"Three."

"Oh, you're kidding."

"I'm not, Beth. Two and a half more, and I'm a rich man."

"Well, what can I do to help, Max?"

"Well, if you really want to help. Crack those 6 nuts and scream into this jar."

[cracking, cracking, and a glass-muted wail ensues.]

"Now what, Max?"

"OK. Now go stand over there."

"OK."

[three days pass]

"It's good to see you, Beth."

"Nice to see you, Max..."
