I created my personal site in late 1994, at the relative beginning of the Web revolution. For this reason, much of the site's coding, filename convention, and other architectural elements are unreflective of the experience I've since acquired.

What the site lacks in graphics and organization, I believe it makes up for in content. Aside from personal information, the site contains:

  • the full text (in both graphical and text-only formats) of three humor books I've written (one of which is linked from the official David Letterman site)

  • the Web's most comprehensive site on the topic of the Cady family name

  • a series of categorized link lists on sport psychology, Web usability, and human-computer interface design which are linked to from some of the lead sites in their subject area

  • other pages on topics such as the Michigan Stadium redesign, baldness, and a holiday known as Fish Day

Since its inception, the site has never had a month of less than 10,000 unique page requests. The content continues to grow.
