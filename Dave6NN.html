Phase III: The Adolescence of My Writing

Pushing the Envelope

Relationships develop over time, and often seem to wax and wane in their enthusiasm. I'll admit that at some point, I began to get a little edgy with Dave. I can't say whether it was the sideburns chafing me, or the fact that watching Dave one night cost me a chance at winning a lovely set of dish towels at the local summer fair (I was told an old woman won them, and didn't even hit the bullseye that squarely). It was one of those two incidents, though, that peeved me, and I guess I got a little carried away.

Dave, for his part, wasn't exactly guilt-free however. I was in the bathroom soaking my head (like my girlfriend told me to do) one night, when I believe I heard Dave say something to the effect of, "Cady, you're such a weasely wimp letting your girlfriend order you around like that" during his monologue. That's not funny. I hadn't yet said anything to him. He was just baiting me.

Therefore, I began to send him letters with a little more meat to them. Something he might not read but one of his letter-opening college interns would, and then they'd share it with the others in the office and titter as Dave walked by and perhaps start stealing one of his shoes right before airtime or something. It was indirect, but would be effective nonetheless. I don't know if Dave ever read one of thse letters - wait, actually I do. He didn't, because I never sent them. That's right.

The Letters

     of, I certainly haven't found them yet.

     outlasts Metamucil." I could berate 
     older women for not wearing Depends.
     Believe me, Dave, I can be annoying.

                    Yours Truly,
                    John

I never liked to get mad at Dave. But just like when I found out that Calvert DeForest is really a brand of lime sherbert, I could get quite miffed about being played for a fool. 

     Dear Dave:

     your paycheck? Come on, Dave; you can 
     spring for real glass.

                    John Cady
                    Ann Arbor, MI

To get ideas for more letters to Dave, I began to study what other people had written in. After a while, it became apparent to me that the letters were mostly written in jest - joke letters, if you will. I decided to try my hand at the joke letter. 

     Hey Dave:

     Boy, have you got a big nose.

                    Yours Truly,
                    John Cady
                    Ann Arbor, MI

For whatever reason, I soon gave up on the joke letters and went back to developing my relationship in earnest. 

 

     Dear Dave:

     On a recent show, I heard you say you'd 
     fight the whole audience. I'll fight you, 
     Dave. 4:30 tomorrow, by the big oak
     tree. Or are you scared?

                   Your Truly,
                   John Cady
                   Ann Arbor, MI

My temper eventually relaxed. I came to realize it wasn't really Dave's fault that he didn't reply; I could have done my part and sent the letters. I gave up the Dave-taunting, but not before experimenting briefly with psychological torture methods. 

     Dear Dave:

     Guess who I am (I'm doing an impression 
     of you right now). Guess who I am.

                    Yours Truly,
                    John Cady
                    Ann Arbor, MI

Just one of those existential head games. Indulge me.

By now, I had been watching Dave for quite a while. I had noticed, a while back, that his life seemed to lack excitement apart from the show. He was complaining a lot about it in his monologue now, so I decided I could think of some fun things for him to do. 

     Dear Dave:

     Have you ever hollowed out a television 
     set, called a repair technician, then 
     hidden inside the TV and talked to him
     when he comes? Being that you actually
     have a TV show, you might be able to 
     pull it off.

                    Your Truly,
                    John Cady
                    Ann Arbor, MI

I tried another angle, too, in case it was public recognition he found himself lacking.
