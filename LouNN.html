Live & Learn
from Uncle Lou

adages from a man whose wisdom knew no bounds

There are some people you meet who just seem to own the wisdom of the world. To others, their vision may appear twisted and surreal, but to you they are prophets.

I once had an uncle who no one understood. Uncle Lou was an ice carver by trade. He gave most of his free time to the model appliances in his basement and to the Sony Trinitron enshrined before his couch. He rarely spoke a word, even to me, his favorite nephew. But occasionally, very occasionally, he would Speak. And his words were words to live by, those words he did emit.

So precious were these gems of verbiage that my brother Ed and I made a practice of writing them down on our visits. Perhaps we knew that someday we'd be able to make a buck off our hard work of transcription. But more likely, we felt that Lou's meager utterances were too important to be lost on kin. So here they are - Uncle Lou's advice to the world. Live by them, that one day you too might build model toaster ovens.

The Words

On Comparisons

"Show me a duckling without any feathers, and I'll show you the tooth I lost when I was six."


On Substance

"There's more to a hot dog than stuff squished inside a bladder."


On Pride

"You can always hold your head up high, if you invent an elaborate pulley device."


On Common Sense

"When the storm is darkest, keep your head up. A flying lawn chair can really clean your clock."


On Experience

"An educated man will give you facts. A learned man will give you observations. An idiot will just babble on and on about what he saw on TV last night."


On Hope

"Hope is the mother of Jenny and Tom. It's Sarah you're thinking of."


On True Beauty

"You know it's true beauty if you look at it and say, 'I'd like that hanging in my den'."


On Imagination

"Imaginary friends are ok to have, until they start wetting your bed."


On Choices

"Where there's a Will, there's a guy who hates the name Billy."


On Constants

"Good times come and good times go, but trees always stay right in the same place."


On Definitions

"Two's company, three's a trio."


On Courage

"If someone makes you scared, you just look 'em straight in the eye and say, 'I'll give you a dollar to go away'."


On What's What

"Laundry may not be fun, but teasing squirrels with plastic nuts sure is."


On Dishonesty

"Stealing from another is like stealing from yourself. Actually, that's not true at all. Forget that."


On Value

"A puddle in the hand is worth a lot, if you can hold onto it and get to a desert really quick."


On Solace

"Misery loves I Dream of Jeannie."


On Wisdom

"The fool licks the lid of the mayonnaise jar. The wise man saves it in his pocket, for later."


On Reason

"An eye for an eye is a pointless trade. You can't just exchange eyes and expect them to fit."


On Rhyming

"Our parents we always ought to thank, but why keep Orca in that huge water tank?"


On Travel

"I once met a man from Nantucket. He said something I shouldn't repeat."


On Humor

"There's nothing funny about a person falling down. There is something funny about a dog in a tree, but I can't remember what it is now."


On Preparedness

"Toll booths are God's way of saying, 'I hope you brought change'."


On Fortune

"Always keep a button in your pocket. You never know but when a millionaire will come up to you and say, 'I'll give you a million dollars for a button'."


On Perserverance

"If at first you don't succeed, try, try again."


On Tropical Fruit

"A coconut can't really be called a nut, because it's hairy. Hairy things are called mammals."


On Philosophy

"Go ask your Aunt Ruth. You're blocking the TV."


On Love

"It is better to have loved something inanimate than never to have loved at all."


On Education

"An education is something you'll treasure always - unless you're a pirate. Then you just bury it and move on."


On Power

"If you think the pen is mightier than the sword, try writing on your arm with a sword."


On Moderation

"There's such a thing as too much cucumber sundae."


On Appreciating What You've Got

"Always be sure to clean your plate. There are starving people in the world, and they hate dirty plates."


On External Praise

"A trophy is just a piece of plastic. Look how much plastic I've got."


On Diet

"An apple a day gets too expensive. Have a slice of Velveeta."


On Climate

"Everyone talks about the weather, but no one actually talks to it."


On Style

"Never let 'em see you pick your butt."
